1: GENRE

 

Your magic system will work best when it is tied closely to the story, the characters, and the world.

 

Think, first, about the genre of your book. There is no rule that says magic can only exist in fantasy novels, and it frequently makes an appearance in science-fiction, horror, and many other genres. The genre of your story can help to inform what kind of magic system you create.

 

If you’re writing a steampunk story, you might base your magic in mechanics, or plumbing, or fire. If you’re writing an epic fantasy, it might be nature-based magic, in urban-fantasy, it might be based on the moon. If you’re putting magic into a futuristic setting, it might be closely linked with technology and science.

 

Write down the genre of your story, and the genre elements and conventions you plan to include. Look at ways in which you can tie your magic system to these things.
